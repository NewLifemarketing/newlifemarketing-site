The Campaign At A Glance

Industry: Restaurant · Fresh meatsServices: Content production, social management, filmed ads, Meta AdsClient since: February 2024

The challenge

Where They Started

Great food, loyal regulars, and an online presence that had to be built from scratch. Restaurants live on appetite appeal, and stock photography can't sell food people can actually smell from the parking lot — so the brief was simple: turn social media into a genuine sales channel, not a scrapbook.

What we ran

The Play, Step By Step

The content engine came first (February 2024): one shoot day a month — at the restaurant, the warehouse, even dockside — becomes 7 reels, 23 carousels and 4 ad campaigns. Then, in January 2025, we put paid behind it: prospecting ad sets running year-round, with seasonal pushes doing the heavy lifting — camper and cottage-country campaigns for the summer crowd, BBQ package sales, a three-week Christmas campaign for the holidays. Every ad built from real footage of real food.

The climb

How The Numbers Stacked Up

Across 97 lifetime campaigns and 1.3M impressions, the three top-spending campaigns did the heavy lifting: Prospecting Q4 ($4,799 spend → $30,174 value, 6.29x), Prospecting Campaign 1 ($3,940 → $44,503, 11.29x) and 2026 Q1 ($2,020 → $20,024, 9.91x) — together $94,701 in tracked purchase value and 483 purchases on roughly $10.8K of spend, from a restaurant that started with zero paid advertising.

The proof

Real Reports, Not Rounded-Up Claims

Meta-reported purchase conversion values (multiple attribution settings across ad sets, primarily 7-day click / 1-day view). Figures reflect the account's three top-spending campaigns (~90% of total spend); remaining campaigns add further value. Tracked value, not audited revenue.
