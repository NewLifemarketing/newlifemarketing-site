Inside the work

From Empty Calendar To Finished Film

Creative production is a process, not a favour. Here's how a shoot day becomes a library of content that feeds every channel you run.

Pre-production

Because the best shoots are planned, not winged

Concept, script or talking points, shot list and scheduling — we plan what we're capturing and the content it will become before the crew ever shows up, so a shoot day produces weeks of content.

The shoot day

We come to you and capture the real thing

Our crew films on-site — your work, your team, your product, your space. Real footage of real work is what makes content believable and ads convert.

The gear

10-bit cinema, not a phone on a tripod

Cinema cameras shooting 10-bit colour, gimbals for motion, drone for aerials, proper lighting and audio. The production quality is the difference between “scrolled past” and “who made that?”

Editing & post

Where footage becomes something people finish watching

Professional editing, colour grading, sound design, captions and motion — cut for the hook in the first second and the platform it's built for.

Formats & deliverables

One shoot, a whole library of content

Short-form vertical reels, long-form brand films, testimonials, before/afters, product spots and photography — all from the same shoot day, exported for every platform.

Built for distribution

Creative that's ready to work, not just look nice

Everything is cut ad-ready for Meta and Google/YouTube, and sized for organic social — because creative is the biggest lever on ad performance.

Straight answers

Creative Production FAQ

The questions we get before a shoot — what we make, the gear, ownership, timelines and cost — answered straight.

What do you actually produce?

Everything from short vertical reels and ad spots to testimonials, before/afters, product photography and full brand films — filmed on cinema gear and edited in-house. One shoot day usually produces weeks of content across formats.

Do you come to us or do we come to you?

We come to you. The whole point is capturing your real business — your work, team, products and space — on-site. We handle gear, lighting and audio; you just keep doing what you do.

What gear do you shoot on?

10-bit cinema cameras, gimbals for smooth motion, a drone for aerials, plus professional lighting and audio. That production quality is what separates content people watch from content they scroll past.

How is this different from hiring a freelance videographer?

A freelancer hands you clips; we build content designed to sell — planned around your marketing, cut ad-ready, and connected to the ads and social that distribute it. It's production with a purpose, not just pretty footage.

How long until we get the finished content?

Typically one to two weeks after the shoot, depending on how many deliverables. Rush timelines can be arranged around a launch or event.

Do we own the footage?

Yes — you get both the finished edits and the raw files. It's your business on camera; it's your footage.

How much does a production cost?

It ranges with scope — a single shoot day is very different from an ongoing monthly content retainer. Most clients start with a shoot day or a monthly package; we'll scope a real number on the call.

Can you shoot for our ads specifically?

Absolutely — most of what we film is built to run as ads. Everything is cut ad-ready for Meta and Google/YouTube.
