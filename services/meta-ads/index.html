The problem

Boosting Posts Is Not A Strategy

Hitting “Boost” gets you likes from people who'll never buy. Meta is one of the most powerful direct-response machines ever built — but only if you run it as a system: the right objective, a real funnel, deliberate audiences, relentless creative testing and proper tracking.

What it is

Meta Ads, In Plain English

Meta ads put your business in front of precisely targeted people while they scroll Facebook and Instagram. Unlike Google, where people search for you, on Meta you interrupt the scroll — which means the ad itself has to stop the thumb. That's why creative is everything.

Run properly, it's a full funnel: cold ads introduce you, retargeting closes the people who showed interest, and your CRM follows up automatically. Ads → landing page → follow-up, working as one connected system.

The strategy

How We Structure & Run Meta Campaigns

Most “Meta ads” advice stops at benefits. Here's the actual strategy we run — the funnel, the audiences, and how we control budget with ABO and CBO.

Campaign objectives

Every campaign starts here

Awareness, engagement, leads or sales — Meta optimizes toward whatever objective you choose, so choosing wrong wastes the whole budget. We pick the objective from your actual goal: a booked call is a leads/sales objective, not a “get more followers” one.

The funnel: Prospecting → Retargeting → Retention

The system behind every account we run

Cold prospecting introduces you to new people; retargeting re-engages the ones who showed interest; retention brings customers back. Ads feed a landing page, the landing page feeds your CRM, the CRM follows up — one connected system, not scattered boosts.

Prospecting (cold audiences)

Reaching people who don't know you yet

Broad and Advantage+ audiences let Meta's AI find buyers; interest and behavior targeting narrows to the right people; lookalike audiences clone your best existing customers. We test these against each other to find who converts cheapest.

Retargeting (warm audiences)

Re-engaging people who already showed interest

Site visitors, video viewers, page and Instagram engagers, and your customer lists. These people already know you, so they convert at a fraction of the cost — this is where a lot of the profit quietly comes from.

ABO vs CBO

How we control budget — test with one, scale with the other

ABO (Ad Set Budget Optimization) sets the budget at the ad-set level for tight control — we use it to test audiences and creative with guaranteed spend on each. CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization) sets budget at the campaign level and lets Meta push it to the best-performing ad sets — we use it to scale proven winners. Test with ABO, scale with CBO.

Audience types

The targeting toolkit

Custom audiences (your data — visitors, lists, engagers), lookalikes (new people who resemble your best customers), and broad / Advantage+ (Meta's AI finds them). We layer these deliberately across the funnel.

Creative testing

The single biggest lever on Meta

On Meta, creative is the targeting. We test hooks, formats and angles constantly, and because we produce video in-house with our cinema crew, we can turn out fresh winners faster than an agency waiting on a freelancer.

Tracking

So you know what's actually working

Meta Pixel plus the Conversions API for accurate, iOS-proof measurement, custom conversions for the actions that matter, and attribution set up so you can trust the numbers in the dashboard.

Straight answers

Meta Ads FAQ

The questions we get before every Meta engagement — including budget and the ABO-vs-CBO one — answered straight.

How much should I budget for Meta ads?

Plan for two things: ad spend plus our management and creative. Spend needs enough room to test audiences and creative properly, but the right level — and our fee — is genuinely different per client and package, so we don't publish set rates. You'll get a real number for your goals on the call.

What's the difference between ABO and CBO?

ABO sets the budget at the ad-set level, giving tight control — we use it to test audiences and creative with guaranteed spend on each. CBO sets the budget at the campaign level and lets Meta send it to the best performers — we use it to scale winners. In short: test with ABO, scale with CBO.

Do you make the video ads or do I?

We do. Our in-house crew films and edits everything — that's the whole point. On Meta, creative is the single biggest lever, so we don't leave it to stock footage or a freelancer. See Creative Production.

Facebook or Instagram — which one?

Both, from one account. Meta places your ads across Facebook, Instagram, Reels, Stories and Messenger, and we let the data decide where your budget performs best rather than guessing.

Why do I need retargeting?

Most people don't buy the first time they see you. Retargeting re-engages the people who already visited, watched or engaged — they convert at a fraction of cold-audience cost, so it's usually where the profit is.

How soon will I see results?

Meta needs a short learning phase — expect the first 1–2 weeks to gather data, with clearer signal by weeks 3–4. The compounding gains come as we test creative and move winners into CBO over the first 60–90 days.

What is the Conversions API and why does it matter?

Since iOS privacy changes, the Pixel alone misses conversions. The Conversions API sends event data server-side so Meta can still optimize and you can trust your reporting. We set both up together.

Who owns the ad account and the creative?

You do. Ads run in your Business Manager, and you keep the footage and assets we produce. Nothing is held hostage if we part ways.
