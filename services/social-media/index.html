The problem

A Feed That's Been Quiet Since Spring

Most businesses know they should be posting, so they do — sporadically, with stock graphics or a rushed phone photo, whenever there's a spare minute. Then it goes quiet for three weeks. That pattern doesn't build a following or a pipeline; it just signals that nobody's home.

Great social isn't about posting more. It's about a plan, real content people want to watch, and showing up consistently — run by someone whose job it actually is.

Straight answers

Social Media FAQ

What owners ask before handing over their social — who makes the content, how often, what it costs, and who owns it — answered straight.

Do you create the content or just post it?

We create it. Our crew films at your business and edits reels, carousels and stories from real footage — then we schedule and publish it. You're not sending us clips to post; we produce the whole thing.

Which platforms do you manage?

Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn and YouTube — planned and published from one place (Sprout Social). We focus on the channels where your customers actually are rather than spreading thin.

How often will you post?

It depends on the plan, but a typical retainer is several reels plus carousels and stories each week. Our BJJ client, for example, runs 7 reels and 20 carousels a month. We'll recommend a cadence that fits your goals and budget.

Do I have to be on camera?

It helps — a face builds trust faster than a logo — but no. We build plenty of content around your work, products, team and results if you'd rather stay behind the scenes.

How much does social media management cost?

Most local retainers run in the four-figure range per month depending on how much we film and post and whether we're managing community and ads too. We'll give you a real number on the call.

Isn't posting free? Why pay for it?

Posting is free; consistent, well-produced content that actually grows a business is a job. You're paying for the strategy, the film crew, the editing and the management — the parts that make it work.

Do you run the paid ads too, or just organic?

Both are available and they work best together. Organic builds trust; paid scales reach. The same footage feeds both.

Who owns the content and accounts?

You do. The accounts are yours, and you keep every piece of footage and content we produce.
