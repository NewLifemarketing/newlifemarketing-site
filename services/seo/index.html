The six pillars

What Real SEO Actually Involves

“SEO” is six disciplines working together. Skip one and the whole thing underperforms. Here's each pillar — what it is, why it matters, and what we do.

Technical SEO

The foundation — if Google can't crawl it, nothing else matters

Crawlability, site structure, page speed, mobile usability, clean heading structure, internal linking, metadata, broken-link and redirect cleanup, XML sitemaps and Search Console health. We also add schema markup — code that tells Google exactly what a page is (a service, a review, a business) so it can show rich results. Get this wrong and your best content stays invisible.

Keyword strategy

Targeting what your customers actually type

We research service keywords, local “near me” terms, commercial-intent phrases, problem-based searches, comparison terms and long-tail queries — then map each one to the right page instead of stuffing everything onto a single generic page. One page, one job.

On-page & service-page optimization

Turning pages into pages that rank AND convert

Titles, meta descriptions, headings, alt text, internal links, CTAs and keyword placement — plus a full rewrite of your service pages so each one explains the problem, describes the service, answers objections, shows proof and guides the visitor to act. Ranking is half the job; converting is the other half.

Local SEO + Google Business Profile

Owning the map pack in your service area

Location and service-area pages, service-area language, local content and local internal linking — plus we manage the Google Business Profile itself: posts, photos, categories, Q&A and a reviews strategy that lifts map-pack ranking. We run this on the Semrush Local tool (see our software stack).

Content strategy

Earning authority Google can't ignore

Blogs, guides, FAQs, case studies and resource pages that answer the questions your buyers ask — building topical authority (Google trusting you as an expert on your subject) so you rank for far more than your core service terms. Produced in-house, not outsourced.

AI-search visibility

Getting cited where search is going

Search now includes Google AI Overviews and LLM answers in ChatGPT, Gemini and Perplexity. We structure content, schema and entities so your business gets pulled into and cited by those AI answers — a modern edge most agencies still ignore.

Local SEO & reviews

Win The Map Pack, Then Compound It

For local businesses, the three-result map pack is the whole game — it sits above the regular results and takes most of the clicks. We optimize your Google Business Profile, service-area pages and local signals to get you there.

Then reviews compound it. We build a review-request system into your follow-up — every finished job asks for a review — so your rating and volume climb, your reputation grows, and Google rewards it with higher rankings and more leads. It feeds directly off your CRM.

Straight answers

SEO FAQ

The real questions owners ask before investing in SEO — including the “how long,” “can you guarantee rankings” and “what does it cost” ones — answered honestly.

How long does SEO take to work?

Honestly: it's a compounding game. Expect early movement in 3–4 months, meaningful ranking and traffic gains by 6 months, and the biggest returns from month 9 onward as authority builds. Anyone promising page one in 30 days is either guessing or gaming something that won't last.

Can you guarantee first-page rankings?

No — and you should be wary of anyone who does. Nobody controls Google's algorithm, so exact-ranking guarantees aren't honest. What we guarantee is the right work done properly against the metrics that move rankings, plus transparent reporting so you see the climb.

Do I need SEO if I already run Google and Meta ads?

They do different jobs and work best together. Ads buy you traffic today and stop when the budget stops; SEO compounds and keeps producing leads for free once it ranks. Most clients run ads for immediate flow while SEO builds the long-term asset underneath.

How much does SEO cost?

It's scoped to how competitive your market is and how much content and technical work it needs, so it's different per client and package — we don't publish set rates. On the call we'll give you a real number for your situation, not a vague range.

What is the “map pack” and local SEO?

The map pack is the block of three businesses with a map that shows at the top for local searches like “contractor near me.” Local SEO — optimizing your Google Business Profile, local pages and reviews — is how you get into it, and it's where most local leads come from.

Do you actually manage my Google Business Profile?

Yes. We don't just advise — we run it: categories, services, posts, photos, Q&A and a reviews strategy. A well-managed profile is one of the strongest local ranking factors there is.

Does SEO still matter with AI Overviews and ChatGPT?

More than ever — it just expanded. Those AI answers are built from the same signals SEO strengthens: clear structure, schema, authority and trustworthy content. We optimize so your business gets cited in AI answers, not buried under them.

What makes NewLife's SEO different?

Three things: we produce content and video in-house (most agencies outsource it), we actively manage your Google Business Profile rather than just report on it, and we run it on a real platform stack (Semrush) with reporting you can actually read. Plus you deal with the founder, not a rotating account manager.

Who owns the work and what do I get in reporting?

You own everything — the pages, content and profile all stay yours. Each month you get a plain-English report on keyword rankings, organic traffic and leads, plus what we did and what's next.
